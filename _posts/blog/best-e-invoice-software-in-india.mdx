## What is GST E-Invoicing Mandate

Since its introduction, the GST e-invoicing mandate has undergone multiple phases, progressively expanding to include more businesses. Initially, the mandate applied to businesses with a turnover exceeding Rs. 500 crores. 

Over time, this threshold has been lowered to bring more businesses under the requirement, culminating in a rollout aimed at businesses with a Rs. 10 crore turnover and above.

**Mandate Timeline and Applicability:**

* **Phase 1 (2020)**: Introduced for businesses with turnover exceeding Rs. 500 crores.

* **Phase 2 (2021)**: Expanded to include businesses with turnover above Rs. 100 crores.

* **Phase 3 (2022)**: The threshold dropped to Rs. 50 crores.

* **Phase 4 (2023)**: Extended to businesses with a turnover of Rs. 20 crores.

* **Phase 5 (2025)**: GST e-invoicing will be applicable to businesses with a turnover above Rs. 10 crores.

#### **The Critical Importance of Compliance**

Failure to comply with the e-invoicing mandate can lead to significant financial penalties and disruptions. Businesses risk:

* **Delayed payments** due to incorrect or missing GST filings.

* **Penalties for non-compliance** under GST regulations.

* **Inability to claim input tax credit**, which could affect cash flow and business operations.

Therefore, staying compliant is not just about avoiding fines; it is about ensuring smooth business operations and tax benefits.

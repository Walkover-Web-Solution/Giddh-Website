## **The Challenges of Manual Bank Account Reconciliation**

Accounting teams are under pressure to maintain accuracy while handling large volumes of transactions. Despite the best efforts, relying on spreadsheets and disconnected systems introduces errors and delays. Here’s a closer look at the pain points accounting teams face when manually reconciling bank accounts:

### **1\. Time-Consuming Workflows**

Reconciling accounts manually is slow. Teams often have to cross-check bank statements against internal records, entering data into spreadsheets or [multi currency accounting software systems](https://giddh.com/blog/multi-currency-accounting-for-business). This process can extend the month-end close cycle significantly, causing delays in reporting and decision-making.

**Why it matters**: The longer it takes to close, the later the business can make financial decisions, slowing down growth and financial agility.

### **2\. Increased Risk of Errors**

Manual processes are inherently prone to mistakes. Even the smallest oversight can lead to discrepancies that need to be tracked down and corrected, often requiring significant effort. These errors can result in inaccurate financial reporting and jeopardize audit readiness.

**Why it matters**: Mistakes can lead to costly rework, unintentional fraud, or, worse, financial penalties during audits.

### **3\. Lack of Visibility and Transparency**

Without automation, tracking reconciliation progress across multiple accounts and teams becomes a challenge. Accountants are forced to manually update and check each spreadsheet, losing visibility into discrepancies or potential issues until it’s too late.

**Why it matters**: Poor visibility means accountants can’t identify issues in real-time, which could lead to unresolved discrepancies at the close of the accounting period.

### **4\. Manual Exception Handling**

[Bank account reconciliation software](https://giddh.com/blog/bank-reconciliation-software-for-finance) often highlights exceptions automatically, allowing for a structured, time-efficient process to resolve discrepancies. But without automation, manual intervention becomes necessary, leading to delays and errors in exception handling.

**Why it matters**: Dealing with exceptions without automation is slow, error-prone, and adds unnecessary complexity to an already difficult process.
